How it works

A customized blend of vitamins and minerals is mixed in an IV bag and delivered directly into the bloodstream. Because it bypasses the digestive system, it can offer higher absorption than oral supplements. At your visit, a doctor will review your history, do a quick physical exam, and determine your suitability for IV therapy. If appropriate, your doctor will then customize an IV drip tailored to your needs. Your infusion is delivered over a controlled timeframe, and your comfort is monitored throughout.

Every visit includes medical screening to make sure the treatment is safe and appropriate for you.

Most IV treatments take 30–75 minutes, depending on the type of infusion and the vitamins included.

Downtime

Your IV can be tailored to your goals, with wellness-focused blends and add-ons used where appropriate.

Safety screening

Before treatment, we review your health history to make sure the infusion is safe and appropriate for you.

Comfort

Most people feel only a small pinch when the IV is inserted, then relax comfortably while the infusion runs.

Before your visit

We recommend eating a light meal and drinking water before your appointment to help you feel more comfortable during the infusion.

FAQ

Questions, answered

Is IV vitamin therapy safe? +
IV therapy is generally safe when administered by trained medical professionals using sterile equipment and approved ingredients. We review your health history before treatment to ensure the infusion is appropriate for you.
Who should avoid IV therapy? +
IV therapy may not be recommended for people with certain medical conditions such as kidney disease, heart failure, severe allergies, or some chronic illnesses. Your provider will review your history before treatment.
When will I notice results? +
Some patients notice benefits the same day, while others notice changes after several treatments. Results depend on your hydration level, health status, and the type of IV you receive.
How often should I get IV therapy? +
Frequency depends on your goals. Some people come occasionally for hydration or recovery, while others follow a regular schedule recommended by their provider.
Are there any side effects? +
Side effects are uncommon but may include mild bruising at the IV site, feeling cold during the drip, or temporary lightheadedness. You are monitored during the infusion.
Is IV therapy covered by MSP or insurance? +
IV therapy is considered a private wellness service and is usually not covered by MSP. Some extended health plans may cover treatment provided by a naturopath.
How do I book an appointment? +
Choose the IV therapy option you would like and click Book Now. You will be redirected to our secure booking system to select a time.
