About

What this service does

Botox is injected into specific muscles to temporarily reduce their movement. By relaxing the muscles that create expression lines, the skin above can look smoother and more refreshed.

Results you can expect

  • Softer-looking frown lines (11s), forehead lines, and crow’s feet
  • A more rested, “unfurrowed” look (without looking frozen)
  • Optional refinement of brow balance (subtle lift effect)
  • Preventative support for deepening expression lines over time

Ideal for

  • Forehead lines + frown lines
  • Crow’s feet / squint lines
  • Strong facial muscle pull or asymmetry concerns
  • Clients wanting low-downtime, natural refresh

How it works

We assess your natural expressions, choose strategic injection points, and use conservative dosing to keep results soft and balanced. Small adjustments can be made at follow-up if needed.

Details

Service details

Clear expectations — timing, comfort, and what’s included.

Time

15–30 minutes. Most appointments are quick and easy.

Comfort

Small pinches. Most clients rate it very tolerable. Ice can be used for comfort.

When you’ll see results

Often noticeable in 3–7 days, with full effect around ~14 days (varies).

How long it lasts

Typically around 3–4 months for many people (varies by person and area).

Aftercare

Keep it simple: avoid rubbing the area, and follow your provider’s guidance for exercise/positioning that day.

Touch-ups

Optional follow-up at ~2 weeks if you want minor balancing or refinement.

FAQ

Questions, answered

Quick answers. Ask us anything at your visit.

How long does Botox last? +
Many people find results last around 3–4 months, but it varies by person, area, and muscle strength. Maintenance schedules are individualized.
When will I see results? +
Some notice changes in a few days, with full effect commonly around ~2 weeks. We can review results at a 2-week follow-up if you’d like.
Will I look frozen? +
Not with a conservative plan. We focus on natural movement and balance — “softened” not “stiff.”
Is there downtime? +
Usually none. You may have tiny bumps for a short time and occasional light bruising at injection points.
Who should avoid Botox? +
We’ll review medical history, medications, and allergies first. Certain neuromuscular conditions, pregnancy/breastfeeding, or active infection at the injection site may mean postponing.
