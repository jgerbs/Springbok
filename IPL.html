How it works

Lumecca IPL (Intense Pulsed Light) is a powerful skin treatment that uses broad-spectrum light to target and reduce pigmentation and vascular irregularities. The light energy is absorbed by darker pigment such as sun spots and by hemoglobin in visible blood vessels, gently heating and breaking them down.

Over the following days, pigmented areas darken and flake away, while vascular concerns gradually fade, revealing a clearer, more even-toned complexion. Lumecca also stimulates collagen production, helping improve overall skin texture and brightness with minimal downtime.

Details

Service details

Time

Treatment time depends on the area being treated. Smaller areas may take around 45 minutes, while larger or multi-area appointments can take up to 90 minutes.

Downtime

Downtime is usually mild. Redness, warmth, or light swelling can last 1–2 days, and pigmented spots may temporarily darken before flaking away.

Comfort

Most patients describe IPL as a quick snap or warm pulse on the skin. Treatment is generally well tolerated and typically does not require numbing.

Safety screening

Your provider will review your skin, recent sun exposure, medications, and any active skin concerns before treatment to ensure IPL is appropriate and safe for you.

Best results

Most patients benefit from a series of 3–5 sessions spaced several weeks apart. A supportive skincare routine can also help maintain clarity, brightness, and long-term results.

Aftercare

Use SPF daily and avoid saunas, hot tubs, and excessive heat for 24–48 hours after treatment. Aloe gel or a cool compress can be used to soothe the skin if needed.

What to expect

Treatment steps

STEP 1

Assess + plan

We review your goals, confirm the treatment area, and align on realistic outcomes.

STEP 2

Prep

The skin is cleansed and prepped, and a cooling gel is applied to the treatment area to enhance comfort and ensure optimal light transmission.

STEP 3

Treatment

We provide simple do’s / don’ts and aftercare guidance for smooth healing.

FAQ

Questions, answered

How many sessions will I need? +
Most patients need a series of treatments for best results.

A common plan is 3–5 sessions spaced several weeks apart, depending on the skin concern being treated.
Is there downtime? +
Downtime is usually minimal.

You may have redness, warmth, or mild swelling for 1–2 days after treatment. Aloe gel or a cool compress can be used to soothe the skin post treatment. Avoid saunas and hot showers or baths for 24–48 hours post treatment. Pigmented spots may temporarily darken before flaking off.
When will I see results? +
Some improvement may be seen after the first treatment, but results develop gradually over a series of sessions.

Skin tone and clarity continue to improve as the skin heals.
Who should avoid IPL? +
IPL may not be recommended for people with:
  • Skin recently exposed to the sun or tanning bed
  • Active skin infections or open wounds
  • Certain skin conditions in the treatment area such as melasma
  • Use of medications that increase light sensitivity
Your provider will review your history before treatment.
What does IPL help with? +
IPL is commonly used to improve:
  • Sun damage
  • Age spots
  • Redness and rosacea
  • Uneven skin tone
  • Broken capillaries
  • Overall skin clarity
It works by using light energy to target pigment and redness in the skin.
Does IPL hurt? +
Most patients describe the feeling as a quick snap or warm pulse on the skin.

The treatment is usually well tolerated and does not require numbing.
Can I wear makeup after IPL? +
Makeup can usually be worn post treatment if the skin feels comfortable.

Your provider may recommend waiting if there is redness or sensitivity.
How long does an IPL treatment take? +
Treatment time depends on the area, with an average of 30 minutes total for a smaller area, up to 90 minutes for larger or multiple areas.
