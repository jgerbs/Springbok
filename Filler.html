About

What this service does

Results you can expect

  • Restored midface or cheek support giving a lifted appearance
  • Enhanced lip shape and hydration
  • More balanced proportions with chin and jawline definition

Ideal for

  • Volume loss in cheeks or temples
  • Desire for more lip definition or symmetry
  • Structural contour goals such as chin or cheeks
  • Clients wanting a refreshed and balanced look without surgery

How it works

Dermal fillers are injectable treatments made with hyaluronic acid, a substance naturally found in the body that attracts and retains moisture. Once placed beneath the skin, filler adds structure and hydration to targeted areas to restore volume, smooth lines, and enhance contours. Results are visible right away and continue to settle over the following days as the product integrates with the surrounding tissue.

Details

Service details

Time

Appointments are typically 60 minutes, including consultation, treatment planning, and filler placement.

Comfort

Numbing cream may be used depending on the treatment area, and most fillers also contain lidocaine for added comfort during treatment.

Downtime

Downtime is minimal, though some swelling, redness, or bruising may occur and can take several days to improve.

When you’ll see results

Results are visible immediately, with the final result appearing after swelling settles, usually within about 1–2 weeks.

How long it lasts

Most fillers last between 6–18 months depending on the area treated, the filler used, and how your body metabolizes the product.

Aftercare

A cool compress can be used for comfort, and your provider will review what to avoid while swelling or bruising settles.

FAQ

Questions, answered

How long do fillers last? +
Results vary depending on the area treated and the type of filler used. Most fillers last between 6–18 months, and gradually fade as the body naturally breaks down the product. The final result appears after swelling settles, usually within about 1–2 weeks.
Will I be swollen or bruised? +
Mild swelling and bruising are common and usually temporary. Most swelling improves within a few days, but it can take up to 1–2 weeks for the final result to settle.
How much filler will I need? +
The amount depends on the area being treated and your desired result. We use a conservative approach where less is more to naturally enhance and balance your features. Your provider will recommend a treatment plan during your consultation.
Is filler the same as Botox? +
No, fillers and Botox work differently. Botox relaxes muscles to soften lines, while dermal fillers add volume to smooth wrinkles, enhance features, or restore lost fullness.
What areas can filler treat? +
Dermal filler is commonly used for:
  • Lips
  • Cheeks
  • Chin
  • Facial volume loss
Your provider will recommend the best approach for your goals.
Who should avoid filler? +
Filler may not be recommended for people who:
  • Are pregnant or breastfeeding
  • Have active skin infections near the treatment area
  • Have certain allergies to filler ingredients, or hyaluronidase
  • Have certain medical conditions affecting healing
Your provider will review your history before treatment.
Do I need a consultation first? +
A consultation is recommended to discuss your goals, review your history, and create a personalized treatment plan.
