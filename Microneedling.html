How it works

The DP4 microneedling pen uses ultra-fine, medical-grade needles to create controlled micro-injuries in the skin. This process stimulates your body’s natural healing response, boosting collagen and elastin production. As the skin repairs, it becomes smoother, firmer, and more even in tone and texture over time.

FAQ

Questions, answered

Is microneedling the same as Morpheus8? +
No. Both treatments stimulate collagen and elastin, but they work differently. Microneedling treats more superficial skin concerns, while Morpheus8 combines microneedling with radiofrequency to target deeper layers. Your provider can help you choose the best option for your goals.
How many sessions will I need? +
Most patients benefit from a series of treatments. A common plan is 3–4 sessions spaced several weeks apart, but this may vary depending on your skin and the concern being treated.
Is there downtime? +
Downtime is usually minimal. You may notice:
  • Redness similar to a sunburn
  • Mild swelling or sensitivity
  • Dryness or tightness for a few days
Most people return to normal activities within 24–48 hours.
Who should avoid microneedling? +
Microneedling may not be recommended for people with:
  • Active skin infections or open wounds
  • Inflammatory skin conditions in the treatment area
  • A history of poor healing or keloid scarring
  • Use of certain medications that affect healing
Your provider will review your history before treatment.
What does microneedling help with? +
Microneedling is commonly used to improve:
  • Fine lines
  • Acne scars
  • Skin texture
  • Large pores
  • Uneven tone
Is microneedling painful? +
Most patients tolerate treatment well. A topical numbing cream is applied before the procedure to keep you comfortable.
