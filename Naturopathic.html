About

What naturopathic care focuses on

Goals of care

  • Understand what’s driving symptoms (patterns, triggers, contributing factors)
  • Build a realistic plan: food, sleep, movement, stress, and targeted supports
  • Use labs and testing when appropriate to guide decisions and track progress
  • Support symptom relief while improving resilience and long-term health

Common reasons people book

  • Fatigue, low energy, burnout, poor sleep
  • Digestive concerns
  • Hormonal concerns and women’s health (cycle irregularity, PMS, perimenopause support)
  • Stress, mood, focus, and overall wellbeing

How it works

We start with a deep-dive intake, then build a step-by-step plan based on your goals and what’s most likely to move the needle first. Follow-ups are used to monitor, refine, and keep momentum.

FAQ

Questions, answered

Do I need a referral? +
No referral is required to see a naturopathic doctor. You can book directly for an appointment.
What should I bring to my first appointment? +
Please bring a list of your medications and supplements, any recent lab results if available, and information about your health history. This helps us get a complete picture of your health.
Do you order labs or work with my family doctor? +
Yes. Naturopathic doctors can order certain lab tests and may work alongside your family doctor or specialist when appropriate. Care is coordinated to support your overall health.
What conditions can naturopathic medicine help with? +
Naturopathic care is commonly used for:
  • Hormone concerns
  • Digestive issues
  • Fatigue
  • Stress and sleep problems
  • Skin concerns
  • General wellness and prevention
Treatment plans are individualized based on your needs.
What treatments do naturopathic doctors use? +
Treatment may include:
  • Nutrition and lifestyle guidance
  • Supplements
  • Herbal medicine
  • IV therapy or injections (if appropriate)
  • Lab testing and medical assessment
Your provider will recommend what is appropriate for you.
Is naturopathic medicine covered by insurance? +
Many extended health plans in Canada cover naturopathic visits. Coverage varies, so we recommend checking with your provider. We do direct billing to most major insurance providers.
How often will I need appointments? +
Frequency depends on your goals and the condition being treated. Some patients come for short-term concerns, while others choose ongoing care.
Do I need a consultation first? +
Your first visit is a full consultation where we review your history, discuss your concerns, and create an initial treatment plan.
How do I book? +
Select the naturopathic medicine appointment option from this page. You will be redirected to our secure online booking system, where you can choose an available appointment time.
