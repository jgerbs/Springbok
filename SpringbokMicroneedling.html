About

What this service does

Microneedling (collagen induction therapy) creates tiny, controlled micro-channels in the skin to support the body’s natural healing response — encouraging smoother texture, improved tone, and a healthier-looking glow over time.

Results you can expect

  • Smoother texture + “polished” skin finish
  • Improved appearance of mild acne scarring
  • Softening of fine lines (especially with a series)
  • More even-looking tone + radiance

Ideal for

  • Dullness + rough texture
  • Mild scarring / enlarged pores
  • Early fine lines
  • Clients who want natural-looking improvement

How it works

A microneedling device creates consistent, superficial micro-channels. We pair it with sterile, skin-supportive products (chosen for your skin) to calm, hydrate, and support recovery.

Details

Service details

Clear expectations — time, comfort, downtime, and what’s included.

Time

45–75 minutes depending on area and whether you add boosters.

Format

In-clinic appointment with skin assessment and sterile technique.

Comfort

Most clients describe light scratchiness. Topical numbing is available for comfort.

Downtime

Pink/red for 24–48 hours is common. Mild dryness can last a few days.

Best results

Often done as a series (e.g., 3–6 sessions spaced ~4 weeks apart).

Aftercare

Gentle cleansing, hydration, no harsh actives for a few days, and diligent SPF.

FAQ

Questions, answered

Quick answers. Ask us anything at your consult.

Is microneedling the same as Morpheus8? +
Not exactly. Microneedling is the classic collagen induction treatment. Morpheus8 is RF microneedling (microneedles + radiofrequency) that can target deeper remodeling. We offer both and will guide you.
How many sessions do I need? +
Many clients do a series of 3–6 sessions spaced about 4 weeks apart, depending on goals like texture, scarring, or tone.
Is there downtime? +
Expect pink/red skin for 24–48 hours. Some dryness or light flaking can happen for a few days.
When can I wear makeup or use retinol? +
Makeup is often okay after 24 hours if your skin feels calm. Strong actives (retinoids/acids) are usually paused for a few days — we’ll give you a simple timeline.
Who should avoid microneedling? +
Active skin infections, certain inflammatory flare-ups, or pregnancy may mean postponing. We’ll review safety and medications at your visit and choose the safest plan.
