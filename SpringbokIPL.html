About

What this service does

Lumecca Peak IPL uses targeted light to improve visible pigmentation and redness for a clearer, brighter-looking complexion.

Results you can expect

  • More even-looking skin tone
  • Reduced appearance of sun damage + brown spots
  • Improved look of redness / flushing
  • Brighter, more radiant-looking skin

Ideal for

  • Sun spots / freckles
  • Redness and visible vessels
  • Rosacea-like flushing (where appropriate)
  • Chest, shoulders, hands, spot areas

How it works

IPL delivers controlled pulses of light that target pigment (brown spots) and hemoglobin (redness). Your body gradually clears the targeted pigment/vessels for a more even look over time.

Details

Service details

Clear expectations — time, comfort, downtime, and what’s included.

Time

Most appointments run 45–60 minutes (varies by area). Includes assessment + treatment.

Format

In-clinic appointment. Your provider confirms eligibility and tailors settings to your skin.

Comfort

Quick light “snaps” with cooling and gel. Most clients find it very tolerable.

Downtime

Usually minimal. Mild redness/warmth is common; pigment may darken then fade over several days.

Best results

Often 1–3 sessions spaced a few weeks apart (personalized at your visit).

Aftercare

Gentle skincare + diligent SPF. Avoid tanning/sun exposure before and after for best outcomes.

FAQ

Questions, answered

Quick answers. Ask us anything at your consult.

Do I need a Discovery Visit? +
If you’re unsure you’re eligible for IPL, please book a Discovery Visit prior to an Initial Visit so we can confirm the safest option for your skin and goals.
How many sessions do I need? +
Many clients do 1–3 sessions depending on the concern (spots, redness, tone). We’ll tailor the plan at your visit.
Is there downtime? +
Usually minimal. Mild redness/warmth is common, and brown spots may darken then fade over several days.
When will I see results? +
Some notice brightness quickly, with gradual improvement as pigment fades over days to weeks. Multiple sessions can compound results.
Who should avoid IPL? +
We’ll review safety at your visit. Recent tanning, certain skin tones/conditions, active infections, and some medications may require postponing or choosing another option.
