About

What naturopathic care focuses on

Naturopathic doctors (NDs) create individualized care plans that blend lifestyle, nutrition, targeted supplementation, and other evidence-informed therapies — with a strong emphasis on education, prevention, and long-term health.

Goals of care

  • Understand what’s driving symptoms (patterns, triggers, contributing factors)
  • Build a realistic plan: food, sleep, movement, stress, and targeted supports
  • Use labs when appropriate to guide decisions and track progress
  • Support symptom relief while improving resilience and long-term health

Common reasons people book

  • Fatigue, low energy, burnout, poor sleep
  • Digestive concerns (bloating, reflux, IBS-like symptoms)
  • Hormonal concerns (cycle irregularity, PMS, perimenopause support)
  • Stress, mood, focus, and overall wellbeing

How it works

We start with a deep-dive intake, then build a step-by-step plan based on your goals and what’s most likely to move the needle first. Follow-ups are used to refine, simplify, and keep momentum.

FAQ

Questions, answered

Quick answers. Ask us anything at your visit.

Do I need a referral? +
Usually no. You can book directly. If you have recent lab work or reports, bring them — it helps.
What should I bring to my first appointment? +
A list of medications/supplements, any recent lab results, and notes on symptoms/timing. If you track sleep, cycles, or digestion — bring that too.
How soon will I feel improvement? +
Some changes can be quick (sleep routines, nutrition structure), while deeper patterns take time. We’ll set clear expectations and track progress together.
Do you order labs or work with my family doctor? +
When appropriate, we can recommend labs and coordinate care with your healthcare team. Your safety and continuity of care come first.
Is naturopathic medicine covered by insurance? +
Many extended health plans include naturopathic visits. Coverage varies by provider and plan — check your benefits to confirm.
